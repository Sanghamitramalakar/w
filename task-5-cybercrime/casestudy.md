Cybercrime Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud (QR Code Scam)

What the UPI/Online Payment Fraud crime is:

This is a type of fraud where people are tricked into scanning a UPI QR code to get money but they end up giving the attacker permission to take money from them.

How the UPI/Online Payment Fraud happens:

Here are the steps:

* The fraudster makes a listing for something they want to sell like on OLX or they pretend to be someone looking to buy something or hire someone on platforms like LinkedIn or WhatsApp.

* The scammer gains the trust of the person they are trying to trick and promises to pay them money.

* Then the scammer sends a QR code saying it is so they can start the payment process or check their account.

* The person scans the QR code, which's really a request to take money from their account.

* The UPI app asks for the persons PIN. The person thinks they are entering their PIN to get money so they enter it and this lets the attacker take money from their account away.

Who gets targeted by the UPI/Online Payment Fraud:

The people who get targeted are usually college students, people who sell things online and young professionals who use UPI a lot because it is easy those who want to make money quickly or sell things fast.

What happens to the people who get tricked by the UPI/Online Payment Fraud:

The person loses the money in their bank account. Because the person gave permission, for the transaction using their PIN the bank usually cannot reverse the transaction, which means the person loses their money for good and is very upset.
