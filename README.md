## Project Overview

I worked on the Digital Literacy project as a Student Digital Ambassador. The main goal of this project is to help students understand and use the world in a responsible way.

In todays world technology is moving fast. Digital literacy is not about using a computer. It is about being able to communicate in a way having a good digital identity using online tools effectively and staying safe from cyber threats.

This project is a collection of five tasks, each related to a part of the Digital Literacy course. These tasks aim to teach students about literacy give them practical skills and help them develop good digital habits.

Through this project I used different tools and platforms created content and thought about my own digital practices. This project is a record of what I learned and a demonstration of my skills.

---

## Objectives of the Project

- To understand what Digital Literacy is and why it is important

- To create an online presence on different platforms

- To learn about new tools for coding working together and communicating

- To develop skills for writing professional emails

- To teach people about cybercrime and how to stay safe online

## Module-wise Summary

### Module 1: Digital Literacy Awareness

In this module I made a one-page infographic to teach students about Digital Literacy. The infographic includes:

- What Digital Literacy is and why it is

- How to use the internet safely

- Useful digital tools for students

- How to have a professional online presence

I used an online design tool to create a nice-looking and informative resource. This task helped me learn how to take ideas and make them simple and easy to understand.

---

### Module 2: Student Digital Portfolio

This module was about creating a digital identity. I. Updated profiles on platforms like:

- GitHub – to show my projects and coding work

- LinkedIn – to build a professional network and find career opportunities

What I learned:

- Why it is important to have a clean and professional profile

- How employers look at online presence

- How having a good profile can help with internships and placements

This module taught me that making a portfolio early on can really help with getting internships and placements.

### Module 4: Email Etiquette & Digital Communication

In this module I learned how to communicate online. The tasks included:

- Writing an email to a professor

- Writing a professional email to an internship coordinator

- Making a Social Media Do’s and Don’ts Checklist

What I took away:

- Why subject lines and structured content are important

- How to be polite and respectful in emails

- Why I should not use language in professional emails

- How to be responsible on social media

This module really helped me feel more confident when communicating formally.

---

### Module 5: Cybercrime Awareness

In this module I worked on a case study about UPI QR Code Fraud, a cybercrime in India.

The case study covered:

- What the scam is and how it works

- How fraudsters trick people

- Who the targets are

- What the consequences are

I also made a "Stay Safe Online" checklist with tips like:

- Never entering my UPI PIN to receive money

- Being careful with payment requests

- Reporting fraud

This module taught me a lot, about threats and made me more careful when doing digital transactions.
